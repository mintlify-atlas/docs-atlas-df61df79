# What is MouseTiler?

MouseTiler is a powerful KDE KWin Script that allows you to tile your windows with minimum effort by moving the mouse just a few pixels. Instead of manually resizing and positioning windows, MouseTiler provides intuitive tiling modes that make window management fast and efficient.

## System requirements

<Note>
  MouseTiler requires **KDE Plasma 6 or later**. Compatibility with previous versions is unknown.
</Note>

### Tested platforms

MouseTiler has been tested and confirmed to work on:

- **Fedora 42 KDE** running Wayland and X11 with Plasma version 6.5.2 and 6.5.4
- **Debian 13** running Plasma version 6.3.6
- **FreeBSD 15.0** running X11 with Plasma version 6.4.5

## Tiling modes overview

MouseTiler offers three distinct tiling modes to suit different workflows:

### Grid Tiler

<img src="/assets/popup_tiler.png" alt="Grid Tiler interface" />

The Grid Tiler provides a popup grid that appears when you move a window. Simply drag your window a few pixels to activate the grid, then move your mouse over a tile to place the window. Perfect for quick, visual tiling.

### Overlay Tiler

<img src="/assets/overlay_tiler.png" alt="Overlay Tiler interface" />

Similar to FancyZones, the Overlay Tiler shows a full-screen overlay with customizable zones. Click a tile to place your window, or span multiple tiles by dragging across them. Ideal for complex layouts.

### Auto Tiling

Auto Tiling automatically arranges your windows in predefined patterns. Choose from carousel-style or stacking layouts, and switch between up to three different auto-tilers on the fly. [Watch the video overview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wKhfUGHrgIw).

## Additional features

### Center in tile

<img src="/assets/center_in_tile_v1.gif" alt="Center in tile feature" />

Keep your window's current size but center it within a specific tile using the keyboard shortcut `Meta+Ctrl+C`.

### Theme support

MouseTiler can follow your system theme or use one of several pre-defined color themes to match your desktop aesthetic.

### Input mode switching

Quickly toggle between mouse cursor and window-based input modes with `Ctrl+Alt+I`, perfect for different input devices.

---
title: Center in tile
description: Keep your window's current size while centering it within a specific tile position
---

The center in tile feature allows you to position a window within a tile location without resizing it. This is perfect for when you want to maintain a window's specific dimensions but center it in a particular area of your screen.

## How it works

Instead of resizing a window to fill the entire tile (standard tiling behavior), center in tile mode places the window at the center of the selected tile while preserving its current dimensions.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Standard tiling" icon="maximize">
    Window is resized to completely fill the selected tile area.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Center in tile" icon="circle-dot">
    Window keeps its current size and is centered within the selected tile area.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Keyboard shortcut

Toggle center in tile mode on or off:

<Note>
**Default shortcut**: `Meta+Ctrl+C`

You can customize this shortcut in MouseTiler settings using the `shortcutCenterInTile` option.
</Note>

When center in tile mode is active, you'll see visual indicators in the tiler interface showing that your window will be centered rather than resized.

## Usage example

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable center in tile mode">
    Press `Meta+Ctrl+C` to activate center in tile mode. You'll see a visual indicator in the tiler interface.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Move your window">
    Click and drag a window's titlebar to bring up the tiler interface.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a tile">
    Hover over and select a tile. Notice the hint shows "Center in tile" instead of the normal tiling action.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Drop the window">
    Release the mouse button. Your window will be centered in the selected tile area without changing its size.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Use cases

Center in tile is particularly useful for:

- **Fixed-size applications**: Position calculator apps, chat windows, or other tools that work best at specific dimensions
- **Floating windows**: Create a floating window layout where windows don't fill the entire screen
- **Design work**: Precisely position windows while maintaining their aspect ratios
- **Multi-monitor setups**: Center small windows within larger screen sections

## Configuration modes

MouseTiler offers several center in tile modes via the `centerInTileMode` setting:

| Mode | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 0 (default) | Center in tile is off by default. Use `Meta+Ctrl+C` to toggle it on temporarily. It resets to off after tiling. |
| 1 | Center in tile is off by default. Toggle with `Meta+Ctrl+C`. The setting persists across multiple tiling operations. |
| 2 | Center in tile is on by default. Use `Meta+Ctrl+C` to toggle it off temporarily. It resets to on after tiling. |
| 3 | Center in tile is on by default. Toggle with `Meta+Ctrl+C`. The setting persists across multiple tiling operations. |

<Info>
Modes 1 and 3 remember your preference (set via `rememberCenterInTile`), while modes 0 and 2 reset to the default after each tiling operation.
</Info>

## Visual indicators

When center in tile mode is active, the popup tiler displays:

- A **"⸰" symbol** in the active tile to indicate centering mode
- A hint message: **"Center in tile - will not resize the window"**
- The shortcut hint showing you can toggle with `Meta+Ctrl+C`

In the overlay tiler, the status bar at the bottom shows:
- **"Center in tile: Enabled"** or **"Center in tile: Disabled"**
- The keyboard shortcut for quick reference

## Combining with tiling layouts

Center in tile works with all layout types:

- **Grid layouts** (2x2, 3x1, etc.)
- **Custom layouts** with percentage-based positioning
- **Special layouts** like split and fill modes

The window will always be centered within the selected tile's boundaries, regardless of the layout complexity.

<Tip>
Use center in tile mode with larger tile areas to create breathing room around your windows, which can reduce visual clutter and improve focus.
</Tip>
